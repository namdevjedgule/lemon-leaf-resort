How It Started

A Promise Made
Over a Bonfire

It started, fittingly, around a fire. Three friends - tired of the same hill-station drive, the same overbooked resorts, the same forgettable weekends - sat at a campsite outside Bhor and asked a simple question: why does relaxing always feel like work?

Six months later, on a five-acre stretch of land in Wadhane, that question became Lemon Leaf Resort. Not a resort that imitates the forest with a few potted plants, but one built inside it - where the infinity pool meets the lake horizon, where bubble domes let the sky in, and where every guest leaves having actually disconnected, not just photographed disconnecting.

What we built is simple in spirit: a place close enough to Pune for a spontaneous weekend, and far enough from it to forget you have a phone.

What We Believe

Three Ideas
Behind Every Decision

Everything at Lemon Leaf - from the menu to the mattress - gets filtered through these three questions before it becomes part of the resort.

Nature First, Always

We didn't clear the land to build the resort - we built the resort around the land. Every tent, pod, and dome sits where it disturbs the least and reveals the most.

Hospitality, Not Performance

We'd rather have one genuinely warm conversation at check-in than a hundred scripted ones. Our team is local, and they treat the resort like their own backyard - because for many of them, it is.

Everyone's Invited

Families, couples, friend groups, and yes - your dog. We built Lemon Leaf to be the resort we wished existed when we were the ones planning the trip.

The People Behind Lemon Leaf

Three Founders.
One Shared Obsession.

Different backgrounds, the same restlessness for a better weekend. Here's who you're actually booking with.

Amol Patil

Co-Founder & Operations

Amol grew up an hour from Bhor and knows every back road, farmer, and fort guide within fifty kilometres. He runs the day-to-day at the resort - the person who makes sure the bonfire is lit on time and the breakfast buffet never runs short.

"This land taught me patience before it taught me business."

Harish Nair

Co-Founder & Guest Experience

Harish spent a decade in hospitality before deciding the industry needed fewer lobbies and more lakes. He designs every guest touchpoint at Lemon Leaf - from the welcome drink to the late-night bonfire playlist.

"A great stay isn't remembered for what it had. It's remembered for how it felt."

Prasoon Dangra

Co-Founder & Design

Prasoon is the reason the bubble domes exist. Obsessed with the idea that a stay should look as good as it feels, he leads every design decision at the resort - from the tent layouts to the lighting around the infinity pool.

"We didn't want guests to admire the view. We wanted them to feel like part of it."
Our Journey

From an Idea
to Wadhane's Skyline

2022

The Bonfire Conversation

Amol, Harish, and Prasoon spend a weekend camping outside Bhor and sketch the first idea for Lemon Leaf on the back of a trail map.

2023

Five Acres in Wadhane

The land is secured at Gat No. 13/4, Wadhane - chosen for its lake-facing slope and proximity to Rajgad and Torna forts. Construction of the first Decathlon tents begins.

2023

Lemon Leaf Opens Its Gates

The resort welcomes its first guests with Glamping Pods and Decathlon Tents, the infinity pool, and the now-signature evening bonfire.

2024

The Bubble Domes Arrive

Prasoon's long-pitched idea finally gets built - the Luxury Bubble Domes launch and quickly become the resort's most requested stay.

2025

500+ Stays & Counting

Lemon Leaf crosses 500 happy guests, adds the Rajasthani Tents, and starts planning a dedicated Kids Zone for the seasons ahead.
